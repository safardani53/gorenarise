Tyrann Mathieu | Early Life, Family, and Education

Mathieu was born in New Orleans, Louisiana, the United States. His biological parents are Darrin Hayes and Shelia Mathieu. 

His biological father has been an absent figure his entire life and is currently serving a life sentence for murder. Darrin was involved in a robbery and was in jail when Mathieu came into this world. 

The same day he was released, Hayes fatally shot a person and was charged and later found guilty of second-degree murder. Nevertheless, the two are trying to build a relationship as of now.

Darrin has always been the first to wish Tyrann a happy birthday through a call from prison. Besides that, his birth mother abandoned him when he was very young, and he has barely heard from her ever since.

As a result, his grandparents took him in and raised him. Hence, the footballer struggled a lot while growing up.

Moreover, he grew up in a tough neighborhood that was filled with the wrong crowd and shootouts.

As a result, Mathieu has endured the deaths of several loved ones and has tattooed 22 crosses on his right knee to honor them.

Tyrann Mathieu | Football Career

High School and Collegiate Career

The Honey Badger played high school football with the St. Augustine Purple Knights. He made 32 tackles, five interceptions, one sack, and one fumble recovery as a senior. 

While in high school, LSU offered Tyrann an athletic scholarship that he accepted and played collegiate football for the LSU Tigers. In his freshman year, he played all 13 games and started one of them. 

Not to mention, he was named the Most Outstanding Defensive Player in the 2011 Cotton Bowl Classic. As a sophomore, the athlete continued to excel and was the Most Valuable Player in the  2011 SEC Championship Game. 

Likewise, he also won the nation’s top defensive honor, the Chuck Bednarik Award. However, in October of 2011, he was suspended after he violated the University’s drug policy. 

In his junior year, LSU head coach Les Miles revealed that Tyrann was dismissed from the LSU Tigers after failing to comply with the team’s rules. Shortly after that, several media outlets reported that his dismissal was related to several failed drug tests. 

After that, Mathieu left LSU to join a drug rehabilitation program in Texas before enrolling for the fall semester. Although it seemed like the safety had his act together, he and few other former LSU athletes were arrested for possession of marijuana.

His arrest deeply affected him and his chances of being a round-1 pick in the NFL, and the footballer decided to turn his life around. The former Tigers performed exceptionally in the NFL Combine and LSU’s pro day.

After his performance, many experts and scouts predicted that he would either be a second or third-round pick. Eventually, the Arizona Cardinals picked him in the third round of the 2013 NFL Draft. 

NFL Career

Arizona Cardinals drafted Mathiew into the NFL as the 69th pick overall. Shortly after, he signed a three-year contract with the team worth $3.05 million.

Moreover, in his rookie season, the safety made 65 solo tackles, nine pass deflections, and two interceptions. However, since he was injured as a rookie in a game against the Rams, he missed the training camp for the 2014 NFL season. 

After recovery, he joined the Cardinals as a backup safety. But Mathieu suffered a thumb injury and had surgery that led him to miss two games. 

Nevertheless, Tyrann was named the starting free safety the following season and made career-high two interceptions and his first career touchdown. His career was taking off when he faced another injury. 

The athlete suffered a season-ending injury when he tore his right ACL. Moreover, he was named to his first Pro Bowl in 2015. 

Likewise, the Associated Press named first-team All-Pro safety while his fellow athletes ranked him 28th on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2016.

After the 2015 season, the footballer signed a contract extension worth $62.5 million for five years. 

In the 2016 season, the former Tigers player sustained a shoulder injury and was eventually placed on the injured list.

Although Tyrann’s contract extension bound him with the Cardinals for five years, he left them after the 2017 season as he disagreed with a pay cut.

Houston Texans and Kansas City Chiefs

After the Cardinals released him, the safety signed a one-year contract worth $7 million with the Texans. At the end of the season, Mathieu had made 89 combined tackles, three sacks, and two interceptions. 

For the 2019 season, Tyrann signed a three-year contract worth $42 million with the Kansas City Chiefs. Moreover, he won the 2020 Super Bowl playoffs with the Chiefs. 

Besides that, Tyrann was involved in a verbal altercation with the Bucs’ quarterback Tom Brady during the 2021 Super Bowl Championship. In a now-deleted tweet, the safety said that Tom called him something he would rather not repeat. 

Recently, in 2021, the Honey Badger tested positive for Covid and was placed on the COVID-19 reserve list. Nevertheless, he has already recovered as of now and is back on the field.
