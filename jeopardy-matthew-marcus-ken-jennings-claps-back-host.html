Jeopardy! host Ken Jennings responds to accusations he made an 'inappropriate comment' to champ Matt

 Posted on July 3, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  602 words  |  Aldo Pusey

KEN Jennings could not stand idly by after hearing it from fans about what they accused was an iffy exchange on Tuesday's episode.

The host tweeted at one angered user to assert his side comment to champ Matthew Marcus mid-clue: "wasn't a hint."

Ken bothered ever-protective Jeopardy! fans across various social media platforms on February 7th's game.

As The U.S. Sun covered upon airing, Matthew, the latest multiday winner, buzzed in on a clue before his 4th victory with $114.2K total.

The last remaining clue of the episode for $400 was under "Literary Title Characters."

Ken read: “Lord Henry tells him ‘to get back to my youth I’d do anything’...except take exercise get up early, or be respectable; Wilde!”

The software developer now living in Portland, Oregon earlier in his run revealed he’s a big fan of Oscar Wilde.

Before Matthew responded to guess the character, Ken teased: “Matthew - he’s your favorite writer!”

Matthew was then incorrect with “Who is: Earnest [of The Importance of Being Earnest]” - contestant Carolyn got it as "Dorian Grey."

Although "Wilde" was displayed in the clue, and Ken seemingly was just having fun with the reigning champ, anything mid-clue is testy territory for game show fans.

As a result, threads erupted on Reddit and Facebook as well, where tough customers parsed through the moment and debated whether Ken should have said anything before Matthew made a guess.

'NOBODY ADDED A HINT'

One hot-headed Twitter user wrote: "@Jeopardy what’s up with giving the contestant a clue before answering?!

Ken, 48, responded: "The last word in the clue was 'Wilde.' Nobody added a hint."

Fans flooded Ken's response and appreciated the host speaking up about what was to some, a murky moment.

One commented: "Bravo, Ken! Kinda surprised this is even an issue. It's playful stuff like this that makes Jeopardy even more fun to watch than it already is."

Another wasn't fully satisfied but wrote: "I see. He got the wrong book title from Wilde. Guess viewers like myself were thrown off by the comment during a contestant’s thought process after ringing in."

Thank you for clarifying, Ken. Keep up the great work."

A third agreed: Thanks for jumping in Ken. But that assumes he caught 'Wilde' in the clue.

And yes it didn't affect the outcome but still feels inappropriate to add something after the clue. You do a great job as host, but honestly, this came across to the average viewer as a hint."

GOING FOR BLOKE

Despite the odd incident, Matthew won the game with $26K and is now the sixth contestant to make it into the 2023 winner's tournament.

So far this season - which is reaching its midway point, only five other contestants have won big enough to qualify.

We're looking at six-day winner and trivia whiz Troy Meyer, fan-favorite Ray Lalonde with 13 wins, former poker ace Cris Pannullo with 21 wins, five-day champ Luigi de Guzman, and four-day champ and priest David Sibley.

Much like the ever-enjoyable Canadian Ray, viewers have loved Matthew's British accent and are curious how many games he'll win.

As for host Ken, the former winningest contestant of all time has been knocked before by fans before for making "too much commentary" on stage.

When a Twitter user called him out for it recently, he clapped back by joking: "Looking into this now."

Co-host Mayim Bialik is switching in for Ken starting on February 20th.

Fans admittedly have mixed feelings about this as well, as many have enjoyed Ken's stay these past five months - and since Mayim has only hosted Celebrity Jeopardy! this season.
