Drake fans convinced he hints at 'sleeping with Kim Kardashian' in Certified Lover Boy lyrics amid K

 Posted on January 31, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  525 words  |  Larita Shotwell

DRAKE'S fans are convinced he hinted at "sleeping with" with Kim Kardashian in a song from his Certified Lover Boy album.

The rapper's followers thought he was talking about the KUWTK star on the track Knife Talk - amid his ongoing feud with Kim's ex Kanye West.

Drake raps in the song: "I f**k with her, and f**k with her, and her / I hit up err and tell him do the err, for sure.

"Voodoo curse, it got him while I flew to Turks / Know the dogs had to hit them where we knew it hurts."

One fan thought "Turks" might be a reference to Turks and Caicos, where Kim went on vacation in January amid her split from Kanye.

"Drake hints at sleeping with Kim Kardashian on ‘Knife Talk,'" the Twitter account Strapped posted, while sharing a snippet of the lyric and a headline about Kim in Turks and Caicos.

A fan replied: "Bro drake and kanye need to grow up it's embarrassing."

Another wrote: "Drake admitting to sleeping with Kim K is insane."

Others slammed the theory, however, with one critic tweeting; "This is such a f**king reach, do you know how popular of a destination that is?

"Yall wanna rush to make everything about Kanye I swear."

Kim headed to Turks and Caicos with her sisters Kourtney, Khloe and Kylie Jenner in late January, as well as her four kids North, Saint, Chicago and Psalm.

Kanye later joined the SKIMS founder on the family trip - although Kim filed for divorce from him just weeks later after six years of marriage.

NASTY FEUD

Kanye and Drake have been feuding for years, ever since Drake appeared to insinuate that he had sex with Kim.

On his track, In My Feelings, Drake rapped about having sex with a woman called Kiki, which is Kim's family nickname.

The Keeping Up With The Kardashians star denied the rumors about Drake in a response to In My Feelings.

Last week, Kanye fired back with his new song, Ok Ok, off of his new album Donda, which is titled after his late mother.

On the track, the father-of-four raps: “Okay, now they got me, wanna rap again. Heal the wound and then you stab me in my back again."

THROWING SHADE

“You the type to play the joke and try to hide your hand, not the type to come around and try to play your friend / You the type to cut the grass and snake your bestest man.”

He went on to not only blast Drake for making the comment about Kim but also to slam his rap game.

“You wanna come in and play with the G.O.A.T,” the lyrics continue. “All you rap n****s sound like me, can't tell who is who / You got no real identity, can't tell you from you.”
