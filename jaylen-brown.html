Early Life & Family

Jaylen Brown was born to Mechalle Brown (mother) and Marselles Brown (father) on October 24, 1996. His mother often refers to him as “Old Man” since he has the demeanor of an old man.

Jaylen’s father, Marselles Brown, is a former professional boxer.

Marselles was the WBU World Champion in 2016, the WBU CAM Heavyweight Champion in 2015, and a Hawaii State Boxing Commission Board member.

Jaylen also has an older brother named Quenton Brown, who supports him incredibly. Quenton played collegiate football at the University of North Texas.

High School & College Basketball

Jaylen attended Marietta-based Wheeler High School and helped his team win the GHSA Class 6A State Championship as a senior by hitting two free throws with 0.6 seconds remaining in a 59-58 win against Pebblebrook.

During his first semester of college, Jalen enrolled in a master’s-level course at Berkeley’s Cultural Studies of Sport in Education program. 

Professional Career

On June 23, 2016, the Boston Celtics picked Jaylen Brown as the third overall choice in the NBA Draft, behind Ben Simmons (#1 pick) & Brandon Ingram (#2 pick).

The American player made his Celtics debut on October 26, 2016, against the Brooklyn Nets in the season opener.

On January 27, 2017, he set a then-career-best with 20 points in a 128–98 victory over the Orlando Magic. He was also chosen to the NBA All-Rookie Second Team at the end of the season.

In the 2017 NBA playoffs, Brown led the team to the conference finals, but they lost to the Cavaliers.

The same happened in the 2018 NBA playoffs, where they faced a defeat again to the Cavaliers.

In game 2 of that post-season game (2018 playoffs), he scored a playoff career-high 30 points that made then-21 years old Jaylen the youngest to score 30 or more points in a playoff game.

Despite the fact that Jaylen Brown had back-to-back playoff appearances (2019 & 2020 playoffs), he was not able to get hands in the title.

First-Ever NBA Finals Appearance

In the 2021-22 season, he scored his career-high 50 points against the Orlando Magic on January 2, 2022.

The post-season went even better, as he got to appear in his first-ever NBA Finals of the 2022 NBA playoffs. But sadly, the Celtics lost to the Golden State Warriors.

Brown and Jayson Tatum scored at least 30 points in the same game on March 18, 2022, in a 126–97 win against the Sacramento Kings. 

On March 13, 2023, Brown scored a season-high 43 points in a 111-109 loss against the Houston Rockets.

Facial Fracture Injury

By the end of the first half in the Boston Celtic’s win over the Philadelphia 76ers on February 8, 2023, four starters were down: Marcus Smart (ankle), Al Horford (knee), Robert William III (ankle), and Jaylen Brown (facial fracture).

After Brown took an accidental elbow to the face from Jayson Tatum, he immediately collapsed to the floor and went to the locker room.

Later, the team declared him officially out for the rest of the game due to a facial contusion. After missing four games, Jaylen returned in the game against the Pacers on February 23.

He scored 30 points with 11 rebounds in the Celtics’ 142-138 overtime win on February 24, 2023.

Bridge Program At MIT

In 2019, Jaylen established the Bridge Program at MIT (Massachusetts Institute of Technology) for Boston’s inner-city children, fostering their interest in science and technology. 

Jaylen developed the Bridge Program because he believes many individuals of color, particularly black people, have much to give the world.

However, regardless of how much money their parents make, the color of their skin, or whatever else, their potential will never be realized.

With Bridge, the player looks for students who have a sense of community, are self-aware, and want to do something to help improve the world.
