Chilling book explores how Hitler dressed KIDS in Nazi uniforms for sick propaganda stunts

 Posted on June 6, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  466 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

SHOCKING photos show how Hitler and his henchmen dressed kids up in Nazi uniforms in a bid to brainwash an entire generation.

Haunting black and white pictures, mostly taken in the 1930s, show toddlers in full Nazi regalia.

Others show a gang of eager teens throwing an enthusiastic salute, and a group of young lads manning an anti-aircraft in the final weeks of the war.

Another chilling snap shows a line of Hitler Youth members, swastika proudly on display, grinning at the camera as they prepare themselves to fight for the Fuhrer.

The disturbing photos, many unpublished before now, are revealed in Paul Garson’s latest book Children of the Third Reich – a fascinating and haunting glimpse into life under the Nazis.

Garson explained: “Following Germany’s defeat in the First World War and the subsequent political and economic turmoil, the country fell under the sway of the Nazi Party, which would take full advantage of the country’s pre-existing and popular youth groups.

“The goal: to re-mould them by teaching an all-pervasive martial mindset based on unwavering uniformity of thought and action permeated by intense ultranationalism, virulent racism and anti-democratic fascist ideology.

“The purpose: to forge a new Germany from blood and iron, which was destined to rule Europe.”

Hitler and the Nazi Party wasted no time in indoctrinating Germany’s youngsters to ensure they were loyal to Hitler above their own families.

Children were offered cash for betraying their parents since according to Nazi doctrine: “Your real father is the Führer.”

And kids were enrolled in groups like the Hitler Youth so they could be indoctrinated from a young age.

From cradle to grave, Nazi propaganda bombarded children with images romanticising war and death for the Führer and Fatherland.

The basic Hitler Youth ideology trumpeted opposition to “decadent” American-style films, jazz and modern international art forms.

Much of its activities focused on outdoor life and physical exercise with an emphasis on militarisation.

Some 1.5million Hitler Youth boys received paramilitary training, including the use of the rifle, while others were taught to fly gliders.

No matter what your principles or political stance, no youth could escape the clutches of the Nazi’s systemic brainwashing.

Garson said: “In 1939 the State had mandated Hitler Youth membership for all boys and girls and as a result could count 7million recruited, or nearly 82 per cent of eligible German youth enrolled.”

Paul Garson’s Children of the Third Reich, published by Amberley, is due for release next month.
