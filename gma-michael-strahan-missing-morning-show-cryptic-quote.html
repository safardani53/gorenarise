GMA's Michael Strahan is missing from morning show after sharing cryptic quote

 Posted on May 29, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  548 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

MICHAEL Strahan was noticeably absent from Good Morning America on Monday after sharing a cryptic quote.

Michael, 51, didn't join George Stephanopoulos and Lara Spencer in the studio - but it's unclear why.

Instead, George and Lara were joined by some super cute puppies on Monday's installment of the ABC program.

The adorable canines were named after characters from the TV series Ted Lasso and were available to adopt via Animal League.

Michael's absence from the studio comes after he shared a cryptic quote on his official Instagram brand page.

The post was a quote by Deion Sanders that read: "If you look good, you feel good. If you feel good, you play good. If you play good, they pay good."

Michael captioned the post: "The man has a way with words. And football.⁠ ConfidenceBooster #MichaelStrahanSkin."

The TV host's followers quickly commented as one person agreed and said: "So true."

"That is so good you have to get along with others and do your job," another pointed out.

A third person wrote: "Great saying," while a fourth simply added: "I Believe This, True."

It comes after Michael teased a career move outside of Good Morning America last month that he said could be the "biggest opportunity" for him.

At the beginning of May, Michael posted a video to Instagram.

In the video, he was riding around in a golf cart on a set while wearing a suit.

A voice was played over the video that said: "Knock, knock. Who's there? Daisy. Daisy who?"

"Daisy me rolling to my next opportunity," the last line read as Michael took the wheel and drove fast around set.

The song Ridin' Dirty by Chamillionaire played over the video.

Michael captioned the post: "Never stop moving and grinding! The next opportunity could be the biggest! #HLYB."

"This trend, as the kids call it, reminds me… what is your favorite knock-knock joke? Some are corny, but some are good. Could you give me your good ones in the comments?"

But he didn't reveal what his next opportunity would be.

MIKE MAKES MOVES

But just a week earlier, Michael had revealed news about a show he was producing.

Michael got fans stoked after he let them know what was happening with his new sports show.

Recently the GMA star, 51, took to Instagram to update fans that his hit series Coach Prime would be back for another season on Amazon Prime Video.

Michael shared the accomplishment on the Instagram account for SMAC-Entertainment, his production company.

He posted a clip of Coach Prime star and NFL legend Deion Sanders, 55, sitting for an interview on The Pat McAfee Show.

And a snap of Deion holding up his Coach Prime jersey, placing them both in a carousel post.

The photo of Deion smiling with his jersey announced that Coach Prime had been renewed for Season 2.

The post's caption further emphasized the big revelation.

"BREAKING NEWS: #COACHPRIME has been green-lit by @primevideo for a season two!" the caption explained.
