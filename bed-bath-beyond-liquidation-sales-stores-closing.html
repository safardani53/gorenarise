Final liquidation sales at Bed, Bath, and Beyond will save you up to 70% before hundreds of location

 Posted on May 30, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  533 words  |  Aldo Pusey

LIQUIDATION sales have begun at Bed Bath & Beyond outlets across the country after the retailer announced a raft of store closures.

Shoppers will be able to grab bargains as prices have been slashed by up to 70 percent.

Hundreds of Bed Bath & Beyond stores have closed since the company announced in September last year that it would shutter 150 of its underperforming outlets.

Then, last month, the retail giant revealed that another 150 of its stores would close as it tries to stave off financial oblivion.

Last year, Bed Bath & Beyond had more than 950 stores across the country but that number is set to be cut to less than 400 following the slew of closures.

Liquidation sales are being held at outlets across the country ahead of the pending closures, Go Banking Rates reports.

Shoppers in New Jersey can snap up items that have had their prices slashed by up to 40 percent.

But, prices could be slashed by 70 percent as the closing dates get nearer.

Bed Bath & Beyond in Elmsford, New York, has reportedly advertised a store-closing sale.

An email sent to its customers read:  “A store near you is closing.

The U.S. Sun has listed the Bed Bath and Beyond stores that have already or will be closed down.

Bargain hunter The Krazy Coupon Lady has claimed that the prices of Christmas decorations have been cut by up to 50 percent in her local store.

This week, a Bed Bath & Beyond store in Coralville, Iowa, closed its doors for good.

The outlet shuttered on March 1 and stores in Cedar Rapids, Davenport, and Sioux City will also be closing down, per the Des Moines Register.

A spokesperson told the outlet that closing down sales will be starting “over the next few weeks and months.”

Bed Bath & Beyond has been teetering on the brink of bankruptcy for weeks.

Its stock plunged by 50 percent last month and in January, the company missed interest payments on approximately $1billion worth of bonds, Insider revealed.

But, the company secured a deal with a hedge fund company in a bid to raise $1billion, according to the Wall Street Journal.

Bed Bath & Beyond had lost more than $1billion in nine months ending November 26 and chiefs have forecasted a further dip in sales.

Reports in January had claimed that the chain was going to file for bankruptcy, but it has yet to do so.

The retailer had raised $225million in a securities sale and CEO Sue Gove revealed the company was taking further steps to cut costs.

A phenomenon dubbed the “retail apocalypse” has hit the sector and affected food chains, fashion companies, and major homeware brands.
