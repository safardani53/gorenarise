You now know pretty much all you need to know about June Shannon.

Of course, the reality stars were then spotted at a casino within 24 hours of their arrest and their fight, so it doesn’t appear as if there’s much bad blood between them.

How does the aforementioned gossip website know this for certain?

We’re so very sorry for putting that image in your head.

Doak reportedly also went into detail about what he wants to do to a certain woman sexually–  and, on another occasion, told a woman he was chatting with that Shannon was even sitting right there with him while he basically cheated on her.

Again: we really think Doak may not be a great dude.

Or at least not a great boyfriend.

June’s loved ones are apparently aware of Geno’s sexting and have been urging Shannon to pull the plug on this unhealthy romance.

Thus far, however, such pleas have fallen on deaf ears.

The Blast even cites a woman named Tina Harry who confirms that Doak somehow found her on social media and started direct messaging her.

She continued the dialogue because she thought it was cool to speak with someone on television.

But the exchanges soon got out of hand, with Doak hitting her up every 10 minutes and offering to pay for her to make a trip to Alabama.

According to Harry, Geno sent her photos of his private parts and even FaceTimed her after she turned down this request to make a visit… with June asleep in the bed of the hotel room where they were staying.

Doak supposedly told Tina he wished she was there with him instead of Shannon.

Blech, right?!?

As previously detailed, Doak has had MANY run-ins with the law.

He was arrested in 1996 and charged with felony burglary, later pleading guilty to the burglary charge and being sentenced to three years in prison.

He was taken into custody in 2009 after allegedly stealing a $500 cargo trailer belonging to The Griffin First United Methodist Church in Griffin, Georgia.

Just a month later, while out on bail, he allegedly took 35 Valium pills in a suicide attempt.

In 2010, Doak pleaded guilty to felony theft by and was sentenced to 10 years of probation, 80 hours of community service and fined $2,500

He spent six months in county jail shortly afterward after he violated his probation.

In 2013, Doak’s probation was revoked once again after he was charged and pleaded guilty to felony criminal damage to property in the second degree and given a four-year jail sentence.

Based on Georgia Department of Corrections records, Doak served about 15 months at the Coastal State Prison in Savannah, Georgia and was paroled in January of 2015.

All in all, we think Shannon could find a better role model for her kids and a more loving boyfriend overall.
