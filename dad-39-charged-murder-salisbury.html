Tesco worker dad appears in court charged with murder of wife and daughter slashed to death at home

 Posted on February 2, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  430 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

A MAN has appeared in court today charged with the murders of his wife and daughter in Salisbury.

Tesco worker Marcin Zdun, 39, appeared via video link before Basingstoke Magistrates' Court.

The 39-year-old father-of-three is accused of murdering his wife Aneta, 40, and Nikoletta, 18, at their home in Salisbury, Wilts.

Today he appeared in court for the first time, less than 48 hours after their deaths on Monday afternoon.

Zdun, who has short dark hair and a beard, wore a grey jumper and tracksuit bottoms as he appeared via a video link for the six-minute hearing.

He spoke only to confirm his identity and pronunciation of his name and required a Polish translator to inform him of what was being said.

District Judge Timothy Pattinson adjourned the case until Friday, when Zdun will make his first crown court appearance at Winchester Crown Court, Hants.

Judge Pattinson said: "Mr Zdun, listen carefully. The two charges will be sent to Winchester Crown Court.

"The next date is June 5 at 10am, in the meantime you will be remanded in custody and that concludes matters."

Police were called to quiet residential street Wessex Road around 2.45pm on Monday, with neighbours claiming they heard screams which grew "dramatically louder".

One neighbour claimed he saw Aneta seriously injured in her front garden screaming "where are my children?"

Locals Nick and Jan Maher quickly dashed to the house and retrieved the girls to keep them "safe" in their own home, ordering Dominos pizza for them and watching kids cartoons on Nickelodeon.

Tesco colleagues of Zdun's said he works nights at the nearby supermarket as a night filler, working on shelves from 10pm to 6am.

Aneta is understood to have been a carer at a home care service in Salisbury while Nikoletta is believed to have been a college student.

The Polish family, described as "quiet but friendly" by neighbours, are said to have lived in Salisbury for around four years and originally from Tarnow, Poland.

Neighbour Gary Smith, a 67-year-old retired Army veteran, said: "The girls were little darlings, they were lovely. The family were all nice to talk to, very friendly and always said hello. Not ones to have a full conversation, but polite.”

This morning a Wiltshire Police spokesman said: "A Salisbury man has been charged overnight with two counts of murder.

"Formal identifications of the two victims are yet to carried out, however the women are believed to be Nikoletta Zdun - daughter of the accused and Aneta Zdun - wife of the accused."
