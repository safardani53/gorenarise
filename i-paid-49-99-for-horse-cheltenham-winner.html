I paid just 49.99 for a race horse and now I have a Cheltenham Festival winner

 Posted on June 18, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  704 words  |  Martina Birk

STAGE STAR led from the front to win the first race on day three at Cheltenham.

The 15-2 shot fended off Notlongtillmay (40/1) and hot favourite Mighty Potter (4/6) to score the Grade 1 Turners Novices' Chase.

Harry Cobden was in the saddle for the Paul Nicholls trained seven-year-old gelding.

Stage Star is owned by the Owners Group syndicate.

The syndicate is made up of a big group of people who have shares in the horse.

Some of Stage Star's co-owners were there at Cheltenham to cheer him home.

And shares in the horse cost just £49.99 per year.

A bargain price considering the prize pot for the race was £98,573 altogether.

Following the victory, winning trainer Nicholls suggested Stage Star could target the Ryanair Chase next year.

He told Racing TV: "He was brilliant.
