Jasmine Plummer is the first female quarterback to appear in the national championship with the youth league’s Pop Warner football team. Her life story is narrated to the public through the movie “The Longshots.” 

Women are not expected to do well in sports because of their stereotypical images. Whatever they would do, wherever they would go, they used to be, more or less, subjected to inferiorities, binary to what men had to offer.

However, the trend has undergone plenty of changes over time. Women’s involvement in sports is escalating as they get support and recognition worldwide for their potential. 

Jasmine Plummer is one of the very few women footballers who earned fame and inspires women to live their dreams.

Plummer is well known as the first-ever female quarterback in the 56th Annual Pop Warner Super Bowl.

She has a net worth of $500k – $2.7 million as of 2023. 

She was born and raised in Harvey, Illinois, and she started playing football as a kid, coached by her uncle. She enrolled in the Harvey Colts as starting quarterback for the 7-to-9-year-old division.

Jasmine was a bright student who always got A’s in her elementary school, Angelou Elementary School. Then, she joined Joliet West High School.

After transferring to the University of Nevada from Feather River Community College, Jasmine played as a running back for Nevada Storm and reached the WFA Pro Division in 2022, where the Storm lost to the Minnesota Vixen in the first round. 

She was a demure child. Eventually, she realized the change in her personality when she became a part of football, for she was assertive while playing, unlike her nature in general.

Hallmark Of Her Professional Career

Jasmine started playing football at 8, and it came to her mother, Cassandra Johnson’s attention; when Jasmine injured her knee while playing, requiring 23 stitches in 2001.

Due to her Uncle’s persuasion, Jasmine got admitted to the Pop Warner program.

She became a household name in athletics in the U.S. for her achievement, firstly as a female athlete and secondly as the first-ever black woman to do so.

The film The Longshots was shot mostly in northwestern Louisiana, most in Minden’s small city. It was filmed at Minden High School and the Webster Parish Alternative School.

Initially, Plummer played as a linebacker, and she would take any football match as a competitive wrestler. Since she was a woman of color with a giant body, her teammates did not first give her a warm welcome.

The negativity did not last long, as they soon began to win matches because of Plummer. This changed their perception and led to a positive and happy acceptance into their team despite her ethnicity (afro-American) and appearance.

She was a 1.64 m and weighed 132 lb., which made her a bit more succeeding for someone of her age regarding her physical power.

Her winning culture was first progressing for the team, prompting her uncle to change her position to a linebacker because of her speed and strength in the field.

Although her mom, Cassandra Johnson, did not support her at all, her uncle Fred Johnson was the one who saw potential in her and coached her for the real matches.

Pop Warner Little Scholars, commonly known as Pop Warner, is the largest youth football organization in the U.S.

It is a non-profit organization that provides American football activities for over 425,000 youths aged 5 to 16 years old in different nations across the globe.

Plummer’s Impacts On Female Football Players

Statistics suggest that in the U.S., the number of girls actively participating in football has drastically burgeoned to more than double what it was in the past 5 years ranging from 5,000 – 12,500.

They claim that they should not be excluded from football or any other game because of the assumption that women cannot, for they are considered fragile.

She gave a new realization and a renewed perspective on girls’ participation in football. She also brought a new approach to football and women footballers by altering the treatment of coaches and old-school football society.

Although Plummer does not take any credit for setting the trend, the fact that she played the role of a tremendous inspiration cannot be overshadowed. 

Even after she made it possible to grab the first-ever female athlete’s title as a quarterback, her mother decided that the game should go back to being a boys-only sport.

Later, she quit the Pop Warner football team and planned to become an athletic trainer and a WNBA (Women’s National Basketball Association) player.

She played basketball at Feather River Community College and then transferred to the University of Nevada, Reno.

Plummer plays as a running back for the Nevada Storm in the semi-pro Women’s Football Alliance. She led the team to a Division III national championship in her 2019 All-Stars season.

In the 2021 season, Plummer earned All-star recognition as American Conference MVP en route to Division II.

After reaching the WFA Pro Division in 2022, the Storm lost to the Minnesota Vixen in the first round.

Tribute To Plummer

To honor Plummer for her exceptional game and for being the first-ever black woman to be a quarterback at such a young age of 11, a movie named The Longshots was released in 2008.

It is an American comedy-drama sports film based on the real-life events of Jasmine Plummer, the first female to participate in the Pop Warner football tournament.

The movie is based on the true-life experiences of Jasmine Plummer and under the direction of Fred Durst.

In the movie, Ice Cube appears as Curtis Plummer (Jasmine’s uncle and coach), who is a down-on-his-luck former high school football star that turns his niece Jasmine (played by Keke Palmer) into the quarterback of the local team named “Minden Browns.”

He gets his grip back when he becomes the team’s coach after the current coach Fisher (played by Matt Craven), gets ill. 

Later, when Curtis became their new leader, the same misfits’ team won and made its way to the “Pop Warner Super Bowl,” which made the small city of Illinois ignite with the spirit the team shared and became the pride and glory of the whole town. 

Jasmine respects her uncle as he is the one who truly cares about her and is very committed to being there in any way possible when she needs a father figure and best friend.

Jasmine looks up to her uncle and honors him by wearing his old jersey number, #11.
