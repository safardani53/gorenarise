JIM Breuer and known as an American stand-up comedian, actor, musician, and radio host.

Born June 21, 1967, Breuer is most famously recognized as a former cast member on Saturday Night Live and was there from 1995 to 1998.

What is comedian Jim Breuer's net worth?

Over the years, Breuer has been linked to a successful career.

As of 2021, Celebrity Net Worth reports that Breuer has an estimated worth of $3 million.

Along with SNL, Breuer has also appeared on the Joe Pesci Show, Uptown Comedy, and has been with Gotham Comedy Live since 2012.

In 2013, Breuer started his own show, Jim Breuer and Laughter For All, and two years later, he also started Jim Breuer Comic Frenzy.

Over the years, Breuer has also starred in multiple movies including, Half Baked, Dick, Titan A.E., Zookeeper, and The English Teacher.

Why did Jim Breuer cancel some of his shows?

Breuer is currently on tour and has dates scheduled from August 2021 to December 2021.

In a recent Facebook Live video, Breuer announced that some of his shows will be cancelled due to the venue's Covid-19 policies, requiring all guests show proof of vaccinations.

"Two quick updates on shows that you may think you may be getting tickets to or you already have tickets to: The Wellmont Theater in New Jersey — not doing it," Breuer said. "Also the Royal Oak Theater in Michigan, due to the segregation of them forcing people to show up with vaccinations ... I am also not doing those shows."

"I know I’m going to sacrifice a lot of money, but I’m not going to be enslaved to the system or money," the comedian added.

He continued on to say that getting vaccinated is "a choice" and he doesn't care about what his fans have to say about his decision.

"If you have anything else to say, I honestly don’t care," Breuer continued. "I have to stick to my morals, I have to sick to what I know is right."

NBC News said they had reached out to the venues in question for comment but hadn't heard anything back.

It is unknown if Breuer is vaccinated against the virus at this time.

What have people said about Breuer's decision to cancel shows?

Following Breuer's decision, many have since taken to Twitter to express their feelings with his views on vaccinations, and ultimately poke fun at his remarks.

Author Kimberley Johnson said that "Jim Breuer and people like him are the minority. They are loud but they aren't representative of the majority. Jim and his ilk will be left behind while society proceeds without them. Bye."

Author John Pavlovitz later joked "I think Jim Breuer is Nicki Minaj's cousin," talking about the rappers recent comments on the virus and how she claimed her cousin's friend got testicular swelling after getting the vaccine, the claim was later determined false.
