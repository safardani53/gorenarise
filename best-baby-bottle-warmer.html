WHEN it comes to must-have baby gear, baby bottles top the list, whether a mum decides to breastfeed, bottle feed or do a combination of the two.

Thing is, babies like to have their milk nicely warmed up, which is why a baby bottle warmer is a useful purchase for parents of little ones.

Follow NHS guidelines when preparing formula to ensure the milk is not too hot as it could scald your baby.

Boil the water and then let it cool for 30 minutes before adding the formula - you should then cool the milk further (this can be done by putting the bottle in cold water) until it's around body temperature - you can test the milk on your arm to check.

Microwaves aren't recommended for baby bottle warming: not only can they heat unevenly and potentially be too hot for baby, microwaving can cause essential nutrients in the milk to be lost, which is where baby bottle warmers come in.

Some baby bottle warmers, like popular, easy-to-use steamer designs from NUK and Nuby, do two jobs in one, doubling as baby food warmers, so they'll come in handy during the weaning stage.

You can also find portable baby bottle warmers which are brilliant for warming your baby bottle on the go. These are typically either hot water flask designs which you mix with formula when a bottle is required, or energy-efficient, on-the-go heating bottles, like the Smartech Sleepy Bottle.

Parents using bottles regularly may want to invest in a bottle-prepping machine, which works like a super-quick and efficient kettle, filtering and measuring the water, controlling the water temp so it's warm but not too hot and creating a bottle ready for baby in just a couple of minutes.

There's even a self-heating bottle on the market now: Yoomi's Feeding System, an 8 oz bottle that warms milk to breastmilk temp at the touch of a button.

How long can you keep baby milk in bottle warmer?

It is always best to pick a bottle warmer with thermostat control, and never keep the milk in there for longer than 10 minutes (unless it has the technology to keep it at the exact temperature required). The other factor is bacteria.

Leaving baby milk in the warmer is a breeding ground for bacteria, whether the bottle is hot or cold. Should you forget a bottle, simply throw it away and sterilise the bottle to start again.

Always check the temperature of the bottle regardless of when you bring it out by testing with your little finger or underside of your wrist.

How to use a baby bottle warmer

While baby warmers differ in size and technology they all fundamentally do the same thing; warm formula or breast milk without the use of a microwave. You simply click the bottle in and start heating according to the individual brands' instructions.

Most warmers require you to add water underneath or at the side which helps heat the bottle. Once the water in your warmer has heated it often notifies you but then you will have to heat the bottle to reach the right temperature.

Are baby bottle warmers safe?

Bottle warmers are much safer to heat your little one's milk than microwaves, which often overheat and create hot spots in the milk.

A baby bottle warmer heats gradually and some circulate the milk to stop hot spots whilst alerting you when it's at the right temperature.
