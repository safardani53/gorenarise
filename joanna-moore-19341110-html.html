Joanna Moore is an American film and television actress who has played more than 80 television and film roles. From 1963 to 1967, she married actor Ryan O'Neal, with whom she has two children, Griffin and Tatum O'Neal. Moore's career culminated in the 1960s. During this time, she appeared on several popular shows including Alfred Hitchcock Presents, Perry Mason, The Fugitive, Bewitched and The Real McCoys. One of her most notable recurring roles was Sheriff Andy Taylor's lover, Peggy "Peg" McMillan in four episodes The Andy Griffith Show, 1962-1963. Moore was a guest star in Western TV shows such as The Rifleman, Wagon Train, Gunsmoke, The Rebel, The High Chaparral and The Virginian. In the 1970s, his career began to decline due to drug and alcohol abuse. Moore appeared on screen in 1986 and died of lung cancer in 1997
