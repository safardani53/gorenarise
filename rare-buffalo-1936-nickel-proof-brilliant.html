Rare nickel sells for $1,956 on eBay - do you have one in your spare change?

 Posted on July 22, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  507 words  |  Aldo Pusey

A NICKEL from 1936 with an extremely low mintage has sold online for nearly $2,000.

The most fascinating aspect of the rare coin, which features a buffalo and Indian head on opposite sides, is it’s one of the so-called “proof” pieces that were minted that year.

According to the US Mint, “proof” coins are the “finest quality” pieces it produces.

There were two distinct varieties of the “proof” coins in 1936: the Satin and Brilliant finish.

The seller of nickel in the eBay listing claims it is the Brilliant finish.

“The Satin Finish and the Brilliant Finish seem to have about the same survivability in all grades combined,” Jamie Hernandez, price guide editor for Professional Coin Grading Service (PCGS), wrote in a description of the coin.

“In high grades of PR67 or higher the 1936 Brilliant Finish Proof Nickel is significantly scarcer than the 1936 Satin Finish.”

When it comes to the Brilliant finish, just 4,420 of these coins were minted, according to PCGS.

The coin in the eBay listing sold for $1,956.06 after picking up 24 bids. That would mean the nickel is now worth more than 3.9million percent of its original value.

The seller of the coin the eBay listing claims the coin is certified by PCGS in “PR 66.”

The coin grading service describes that grade as “well struck with a few marks or hairlines, not in focal areas.”

Meanwhile, the nickel could be worth more in higher “proof” grades.

For instance, the nickel could be valued at $14.4million if in a “PR 68” grade, according to Greysheet.

Currently, there are various 1936 Buffalo “proof” coins listed on eBay in the thousands.

Recently, a Buffalo nickel from 1937 sold for $1,100 online.

But make sure you're aware of potential fakes online.

To avoid this, check the seller’s history and whether or not the coin was certified in the listing.

What's your spare change worth?

Keep in mind that your buffalo nickels might not be the only coins that could be worth a serious mint.

You’ll want to check your change for rare Lincoln pennies, half dollars, dimes, Jefferson nickels, and others because some of these coins sell for thousands.

The most valuable coins are usually those with a low mintage or an error, as they're deemed the most valuable by collectors.

To check out if your coins are worth anything on eBay, search the full name, select the "sold" listing and then toggle the search to "highest value.”

It will give you an idea of the amount of money that the coin is going for.

You can either choose to sell the coin on eBay or through a specialist site such as Coinappraiser or USA Coin Book.
