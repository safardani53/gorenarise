IF you are looking to stock up on presents for Christmas and birthdays, you may wish to head down to Lidl.

Lidl shoppers are getting excited for their huge toy sale which is taking place from Thursday October 26 to Wednesday November 1.

Their “Wonder of Wooden Toys” event will take place in stores and feature over 120 Playtive toys.

And the best part is prices start as low as £3.99 for a Wooden Learning Toy, with a Lidl Pluscard.

Also included in the range will be a £49.99 play kitchen, a £29.99 wooden coffee bar and a £4.99 shopping basket.

For kids who love dolls, there is a £29.99 dolls house and animal lovers can enjoy a £39.99 wooden vets.

Meanwhile, you can get two items for £10 in their toy train collection.

Many parents seemed keen to stock up on the toys ahead of Christmas.

Taking to the Facebook group Extreme Couponing and Bargains UK, one mum said: “Deffo going.”

Another added: “Need to go.”

A third wrote: “those trains are a bargain.”

The event may be a week away, but Aldi is currently hosting their own toy event.

Aldi’s iconic wooden toy range has returned this year and shoppers can find festive favourites plus one controversial new addition.

The budget supermarket may risk angering M&S after it unveiled a toy range which features a toy version of Aldi's Cuthbert the Caterpillar cake.

To complete the kitchen experience, Aldi's Wooden Kitchen Set (£9.99) includes coffee cups, a teapot and coasters.

Budding bakers can pick up the Wooden Toy Kitchen (£34.99) which is the perfect place to whip up a culinary storm complete with a sink, tap, washing machine, microwave, oven, hobs and a clock.

The mini-me Wooden Supermarket (£29.99) is also back this year complete with a wooden till, moving conveyor belt and realistic accessories such as Aldi check-out bags and play money.

Shoppers can keep it fully stocked with the Wooden Grocery set (£9.99), including wooden sandwiches, soft cheese and coffee.

All wooden toys will be available in stores from October 19 but only for as long as stock lasts.
